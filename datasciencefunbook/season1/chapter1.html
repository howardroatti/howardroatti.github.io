If you've ever wondered what data scientists do, here's a quick summary: we take messy data, clean it, analyze it, build models, and then try to convince people that our 95% accurate predictions are somehow better than gut feelings.

At its core, data science is about storytelling — except our stories are written in Python, SQL, and the occasional desperate Google search.

People think we build AI to predict the future. Actually, we spend 80% of the time cleaning data and 20% debugging why it’s not running.

“Garbage in, garbage out”? That’s our life. The model isn’t broken — the 50 spellings of “California” are.

Still, data science is amazing. It’s detective work: finding patterns, making sense of chaos, and impressing people with charts.

And nothing beats the feeling when code works after 3 hours of pain (even if you don’t know why).

So next time, instead of “can you build an AI?”, just buy us a coffee.

💻 Hands-on Mini Project: 'Customer Satisfaction – Dirty Data Edition'

import pandas as pd
import numpy as np

df = pd.read_csv("customer_satisfaction_dirty.csv")
print(df.info())
print(df.describe(include='all'))

df['rating'] = df['rating'].replace({'bad': 1, 'average': 3, 'good': 5}).astype(float)
df['rating'].fillna(df['rating'].median(), inplace=True)

df['state'] = df['state'].str.upper().str.strip().replace({
    'CALIFORNIA': 'CA', 'CALIF.': 'CA', 'CA ': 'CA'
})

df['response_date'] = pd.to_datetime(df['response_date'], errors='coerce')

q1 = df['time_on_site'].quantile(0.25)
q3 = df['time_on_site'].quantile(0.75)
iqr = q3 - q1
lower = q1 - 1.5 * iqr
upper = q3 + 1.5 * iqr
df = df[(df['time_on_site'] >= lower) & (df['time_on_site'] <= upper)]

df = df[(df['age'] >= 0) & (df['age'] <= 120)]

✅ Lessons Learned

  • Real data is never clean out of the box.
  • Standardizing values and formats is critical.
  • Cleaning data is where the magic begins.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Filling NaNs with the mean without checking distribution.
  • Ignoring inconsistent categorical labels.
  • Trusting Excel exports blindly.

🎯 Practice Challenge
