🔥 Data Science: When Everything Is a Priority

Season 1 — Episode 13

🎙️ Original Episode

“This metric is critical.”
“That one is also urgent.”
“And this model needs to go live tomorrow.”

Suddenly, you’re juggling 7 dashboards, 3 stakeholders, and a partridge in a pear tree.

The output drops. The quality slips. The team burns out.

Focus is not a luxury. It’s a necessity.

  • ✳ Prioritize based on business impact.
  • ✳ Say no to nice-to-haves.
  • ✳ Protect deep work.

Because if everything is a priority… nothing is.

🔍 Let’s Go Deeper…

The illusion of parallel urgency is one of the greatest threats to productivity in data teams.

We often confuse being busy with being impactful, and without focus, both the business and the models suffer.

🔄 Priority Chaos in Practice

  • Vague goals: Metrics multiply without alignment
  • Constant interruptions: Analytical depth is sacrificed
  • Undefined success criteria: You “finish” work but no one knows what changed
  • Pressure from all sides: Priorities shift weekly — or hourly

💻 Hands-on – Simple Prioritization Framework

import pandas as pd
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Example initiatives
data = {
    'task': ['Refactor Model A', 'Dashboard for Ops', 'Fix KPI logic', 'Build New ETL'],
    'impact': [8, 6, 9, 5],
    'effort': [6, 4, 2, 7]
}
df = pd.DataFrame(data)

# Plotting
plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))
plt.scatter(df['effort'], df['impact'])

for i in range(len(df)):
    plt.text(df['effort'][i]+0.1, df['impact'][i], df['task'][i])

plt.xlabel('Effort')
plt.ylabel('Impact')
plt.title('Prioritization Matrix')
plt.grid(True)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

✅ Lessons Learned

  • Not everything urgent is important
  • Tradeoffs are unavoidable — acknowledge them openly
  • A clear “no” is more productive than a vague “yes”

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Prioritizing based on who shouts the loudest
  • Assigning everything to everyone
  • Using Jira as a to-do list, not a strategic map

🎯 Practice Challenge

  • Review your current week’s task list
  • Categorize each item by urgency and impact
  • Pick three key tasks and communicate what you won’t do — and why
  • Track results: what changed by focusing?

📎 Tools & Resources

  • Eisenhower Matrix or RICE scoring
  • Book: “Essentialism” by Greg McKeown
  • Blog: “How to Say No Without Saying No” – Atlassian

🧠 Final Thought

Data work without prioritization isn’t productive — it’s just reactive.
If everything is on fire, maybe it’s time to build firewalls.
