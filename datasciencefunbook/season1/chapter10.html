📧 Data Science: The Meeting That Could Have Been an Email

Season 1 — Episode 10

🎙️ Original Episode

Agenda: “Let’s align on the data strategy.”

10 people join. 3 speak. 2 open ChatGPT. 1 updates a dashboard.

45 minutes later, we’ve aligned on nothing.

Everyone leaves with a slightly different idea of what’s happening.

Sometimes, the bottleneck in data projects isn’t the data. It’s communication.

A single well-written Slack message can save an hour-long meeting.
A shared Notion doc beats three weekly standups.
A well-documented dashboard is better than 12 follow-up calls.

Communicate like your models depend on it. Because they kinda do.

🔍 Let’s Go Deeper…

Data Science is a team sport. And like any team sport, communication matters more than it gets credit for.

Misalignment around KPIs, definitions, timelines, or expectations leads to bad decisions — no matter how “good” your model is.

🗺️ What Kills Alignment in Data Projects?

  • Ambiguous requirements: Model solves the wrong problem
  • Undefined ownership: Duplicated effort or missing pieces
  • Outdated documentation: Nobody knows what's live or deprecated
  • Communication silos: Conflicting numbers across teams
  • Unclear success metrics: Endless iteration without business value

💻 Hands-on – Improving Communication in Data Workflows

  • Before meetings: Share a clear agenda. Define expected outcomes. Send supporting data upfront.
  • During meetings: Assign someone to take notes. Summarize decisions and next steps live.
  • After meetings: Share action items publicly. Update docs or dashboards with what changed.

✅ Lessons Learned

  • Most model issues are rooted in miscommunication, not math.
  • Meeting quality matters more than meeting frequency.
  • Docs, dashboards, and async updates scale better than calls.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Assuming everyone interprets metrics the same way
  • Letting stakeholders 'drive by' with vague asks
  • Not writing anything down — the silent killer of progress

🎯 Practice Challenge

  1. Review how often you're in meetings vs async work
  2. Rewrite one recent meeting as an email or document
  3. Create a 1-page summary: goal, current status, next steps
  4. Share with your team and ask: “Can we use this format instead?”

📎 Tools & Resources

  • Notion, Confluence, Coda, Slack threads
  • Book: “Made to Stick” by Chip & Dan Heath
  • Blog: “Communication Debt in Engineering” – GitLab Handbook

🧠 Final Thought

Every time you save a meeting, an analyst gets their wings.
Be the async hero your data team deserves.
