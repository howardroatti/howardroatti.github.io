👻 Data Science: The Tale of the Phantom Metric

Season 1 — Episode 11

🎙️ Original Episode

There’s a number on the dashboard.
Nobody knows where it came from.
Nobody knows who owns it.
Nobody knows if it’s right.

And yet… it’s in the slide deck.
It’s being tracked weekly.
It’s driving decisions.

That, my friends, is a phantom metric.

They start as experiments, drafts, or proxies.
Then someone screenshots it.
Then it gets copy-pasted.
Then it never dies.

📌 Tip: Ask “What decision does this support?”
If nobody knows, it might be time to let it go.

🔍 Let’s Go Deeper…

Phantom metrics are indicators that outlived their purpose — or worse, never had a purpose to begin with.

They clutter dashboards, dilute focus, and erode trust in analytics when people realize: “Wait, we’ve been tracking that… why?”

🧟 How Phantom Metrics Are Born

  • Prototype: A quick data pull to explore an idea
  • Screenshot: Someone shares it in Slack or a slide
  • Copy-paste: It appears in other dashboards or decks
  • Institutionalized: Nobody questions it anymore

💻 Hands-on – Finding & Retiring Phantom Metrics

metrics = [
    {"name": "Conversion Rate v3", "owner": None, "used_in_decision": False},
    {"name": "NPS delta", "owner": "Growth Team", "used_in_decision": True},
    {"name": "Page Views – All Traffic", "owner": None, "used_in_decision": False}
]

import pandas as pd
df = pd.DataFrame(metrics)

# Filter out phantom metrics
phantoms = df[df["used_in_decision"] == False]
print("Phantom Metrics to Review:")
print(phantoms)

✅ Lessons Learned

  • Every metric must have a decision attached to it.
  • Dashboards are not museums — they are control panels.
  • Fewer, better metrics beat more, questionable ones.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Keeping legacy metrics “just in case”
  • Failing to define ownership or purpose
  • Not documenting assumptions behind calculations

🎯 Practice Challenge

  • Pick any dashboard with 5+ metrics
  • For each one, answer:
    • Who owns it?
    • What decision is it tied to?
    • When was it last used for action?
  • Remove or archive metrics that fail the test

🧠 Final Thought

Just because it’s on the dashboard doesn’t mean it’s helping.
Haunt your metrics before they haunt you.
